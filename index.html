About Us

Empowering Medical Officers to Write Scientific History

The Newlands Clinic, based in Zimbabwe, is an internationally respected model of excellence in outpatient HIV clinical care. While patient care remains at our core, active, localized clinical research is critical to shaping future healthcare models.

This Research Masterclass was born out of a strategic vision: to bridge the gap between day-to-day patient observations and peer-reviewed international scientific publication. By providing clinician-focused training, we translate real-world clinical experience into solid epidemiological evidence.

Academic Foundations

Focusing on sound core research theory, clinical study designs, and population epidemiology.

Systematic Data Handling

Learning structured methodologies to clean, filter, and organize clinical datasets.

Research Focus Areas
  • Epidemiology: Define precise clinical cohorts, cases, and measures of occurrence.
  • Inference Statistics: Master T-tests, Chi-squares, and confidence parameters.
  • Data Wrangling: Parse, clean, and map clinical trial datasets into clean scientific templates.
Curriculum Roadmap

Three Pillars of Medical Science

Our syllabus is structured chronologically, advancing candidates from epidemiological framework theories up to independent clinical descriptive reports.

01
M1

Fundamentals of Epidemiology

Weeks 1 - 4 • Principles

Master the foundational principles of population health research. Learn to formulate research questions, design appropriate studies, and calculate key measures of effect.

Key Syllabus Topics:
  • Clinical Medicine Foundations & Scope
  • Defining Study Cases & Target Populations
  • Measures of Disease Occurrence (Incidence/Mortality)
  • Leveraging Routine Health Data securely
  • Measures of Effect (Ratios & Attributable Fractions)
  • Observational & Experimental Study Designs
02
M2

Statistics for Epidemiology

Weeks 5 - 8 • Assessment

Develop statistical literacy to analyze research data and interpret published findings. Learn to choose appropriate tests, understand p-values, and avoid common errors.

Key Syllabus Topics:
  • Statistics Intro: Data Types & Descriptives
  • Creating Effective Scientific Tables & Figures
  • Probability Distributions (Normal & Binomial)
  • Statistical Inference & Hypothesis Principles
  • Comparing Groups: T-Tests & Chi-Square Tests
  • Correlation, Risk Ratios, Odds Ratios & Confounding
03
M3

Introduction to Data Analysis

Weeks 9 - 12 • Data Mastery

Develop hands-on proficiency in descriptive statistics and clinical dataset structuring. Clean, parse, and structure research datasets to produce publication tables.

Key Syllabus Topics:
  • Data Import & Preparation (Wrangling)
  • Summary Statistics (Central Tendency & Dispersion)
  • Medical Visualisation (Charts & Graphical Summaries)
  • Cohort Cross-Tabulations & baseline Table 1

Eligibility Scope
